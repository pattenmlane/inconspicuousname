Frankfurt Figure 6a — “Volatility smile” (scatter v_t vs m_t, colored by strike,
                 black fitted parabola through the cloud)
  OUR FILE:     frankfurt_fig06a_volatility_smile.png
                (ALL strikes 4000–6500; wings look noisier — see note below.)
  NEAR-ATM:     frankfurt_fig06a_volatility_smile_near_money.png
                (Scatter ONLY strikes 5000–5500; black parabola fit on ALL strikes’ pooled
                cloud after extrinsic filter — then axes zoom to near-money percentiles.
                See frankfurt_fig06a_visual_notes.txt for why Frankfurt looks cleaner.)
  SCRIPT:       frankfurt_style_plots.py → figure_6a(), figure_6a_near_money()
  DATA:         Pooled over historical day=2 (DTE=6), all timestamps × strikes.
  DETAILS:      m_t = log(S/K); v_t = Black–Scholes IV from voucher mid; parabola =
                quadratic fit to pooled (m_t, v_t) after dropping rows with
                extrinsic < 0.25 (similar spirit to their “disregard bottom-left
                outliers” note).

  WHY THEIRS LOOKS “CLEANER” (ours vs Frankfurt screenshot)
  - They had only FIVE strikes, all fairly close to a ~10k spot, so m_t stays in a
    tight band and IV does not explode from penny-option math.
  - We have TEN strikes including deep ITM (4000) and lottery OTM (6500): tiny price
    moves → huge IV swings → vertical streaks and a tall y-axis.
  - They explicitly dropped low-extrinsic points that distort IV; we already drop
    extrinsic < 0.25 but wings can still be unstable.
  - YES — for STRUCTURE near the money, it is reasonable to IGNORE or down-weight
    strikes “too far” from spot when fitting/visualizing the smile; keep trading /
    risk awareness for wings separate. Use the near-money PNG when you want the
    Frankfurt-like diagnostic; keep the full PNG for tail risk context.
