METHODOLOGY — HOW WE DERIVED EACH BOT
--------------------------------------

STEP 1 — Raw offsets show there is structure
  Compute (price - true_fv) for each level across 1000 ticks:
    bid1: mean=-2.504  std=0.561   (always present)
    bid2: mean=-3.306  std=0.300   (present 542/1000)
    ask1: mean=+2.479  std=0.704   (always present)
    ask2: mean=+3.311  std=0.267   (present 538/1000)
  Standard deviations well under 1 tick. These are bots.

STEP 2 — Integer offset distributions reveal two distinct clusters
  bid1: {-3: 567, -2: 389, -1: 13, 0: 30, -5: 1}
        → two clusters at -2 and -3, plus near-zero outliers (Bot C)
  ask1: {+3: 594, +2: 365, -1: 27, 0: 14}
        → two clusters at +2 and +3, near-zero outliers
  bid2: {-3: 416, -4: 110, -2: 14, -1: 1, -6: 1}
        → mainly -3 and -4
  ask2: {+3: 385, +4: 134, +2: 19}
        → mainly +3 and +4

STEP 3 — Bot A bid formula: the -0.1 boundary trick
  Three candidate formulas tested vs all Bot A ticks (offset ∈ {-2, -3}):
    floor(FV) - 2              →  85.7%  (misses all frac < 0.1 ticks)
    round(FV) - 2              →  40.6%
    floor(FV - 0.1) - 2       →  99.7%  ← WINNER
  Failure analysis: 96/96 failures at frac < 0.1 use floor(FV)-2 but bot gives floor(FV)-3.
  The formula floor(FV - 0.1) - 2 handles this:
    frac < 0.1:  floor(FV - 0.1) = floor(FV) - 1 → bid = floor(FV) - 3
    frac ≥ 0.1:  floor(FV - 0.1) = floor(FV)     → bid = floor(FV) - 2
  Remaining 3 failures all at frac ∈ (0.100, 0.102) — floating point boundary.

STEP 4 — Bot A ask formula: symmetric -0.1 / +0.1 boundary
  Failure analysis for ask: 103/103 failures at frac > 0.9, error = +1.
  Formula ceil(FV + 0.1) + 2:
    frac < 0.9:  ceil(FV + 0.1) = ceil(FV)     → ask = floor(FV) + 3
    frac ≥ 0.9:  ceil(FV + 0.1) = floor(FV) + 2 → ask = floor(FV) + 4
  Accuracy: 99.8%  (2 remaining failures at frac ∈ (0.896, 0.900) — boundary noise)

STEP 5 — The frac-boundary origin
  This ±0.1 boundary is more extreme than hydrogel's ±0.5 boundary.
  Likely explanation: VEV FV values often have small fractional parts (many ticks have
  frac ≈ 0.00–0.10 because the price generator uses discrete steps).
  The bot uses a "tolerance band" — when FV is within 0.1 of an integer, it rounds
  differently than the pure floor/ceil rule.
  The boundary errors (5 total out of 1000) all occur at frac ≈ 0.10 or 0.90,
  consistent with floating-point imprecision in IMC's server.

STEP 6 — Bot A volume: independent draws per side
  Filter to Bot A-only ticks (frac ∈ [0.1, 0.65), vol in [5, 75]):
    Bid vol distribution [15-25]: {15:~50, 16:~50, ..., 25:~50} — uniform
    Chi-squared uniformity p-value = 0.052 (borderline, n=501)
    Ask vol distribution: similar range [15-25]
  Bid == Ask same tick: only 80.1%  (vs hydrogel's 97.5%)
  Conclusion: Bot A draws vol INDEPENDENTLY for bid and ask, unlike hydrogel.
  Rule: bid_vol ~ U[15, 25], ask_vol ~ U[15, 25], drawn independently each tick.

STEP 7 — Bot B offset alternates between -3 and -4
  bid2 offset: -3 (416 ticks) and -4 (110 ticks). Condition on frac:
    offset = -3: frac ∈ (0.1, 0.5) — all 416 ticks  (rounded down, frac < 0.5)
    offset = -4: frac ∈ (0.5, 0.7) — all 110 ticks  (rounded down, frac > 0.5)
  Bot B always places bid at floor(FV) - 3. The integer offset is floor(-(3+frac)):
    frac < 0.5 → rounds to -3; frac > 0.5 → rounds to -4.
  This is the SAME mechanism as hydrogel Bot B, just with a -3 offset instead of -10.

STEP 8 — Bot B conditional presence: the single-sided quoting pattern
  Bot B is only present at level 2 54.2% of the time.
  Cross-tabbing presence by frac reveals a perfect asymmetric pattern:
    bid2 AND ask2 both present: 273 ticks → frac ∈ [0.35, 0.65)
    ONLY bid2 present:          269 ticks → frac ∈ [0.10, 0.35)
    ONLY ask2 present:          265 ticks → frac ∈ [0.65, 0.90)
    NEITHER present:            193 ticks → frac ∈ [0.0, 0.1) or [0.9, 1.0)

  Explanation for "neither" ticks: when frac < 0.1, Bot A bid = floor(FV)-3 = Bot B bid.
  Bot B and Bot A post at the SAME PRICE — they aggregate at level 1 (no level 2).
  On these ticks, Bot B does not quote ask either. Symmetric for frac > 0.9 on the ask side.

  Explanation for single-sided quoting: Bot B only quotes the side where its price is
  "close" to FV. At frac = 0.2, Bot B bid = floor(FV)-3 is 3.2 ticks below FV (acceptable).
  But Bot B ask = ceil(FV)+3 = floor(FV)+4 is 3.8 ticks above FV (too wide, not quoted).
  At frac = 0.8, the logic reverses. Threshold appears at frac ≈ 0.35/0.65.

STEP 9 — Level 1 volume bimodality explained
  When Bot B is at level 2: bid_vol_1 = Bot A vol alone ~ U[15, 25], mean=20.
  When Bot B merges at level 1 (frac boundary or "neither"):
    bid_vol_1 = Bot A vol + Bot B vol ~ U[15,25] + U[30,50] = U[45,75], mean=60.
  This perfectly explains the observed bimodal distribution with a gap from 26 to 44.
  Confirmed: Bot A+B coincide ticks → mean vol 59.8, range [47,74] ✓
