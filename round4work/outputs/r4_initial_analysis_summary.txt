Round 4 — initial summary / analysis (counterparty IDs + bot structure)
======================================================================

Source: round4description.txt, Prosperity4Data/ROUND_4 trades (days 1–3), and
prior Round 3 “insider” style work (inferred flow without buyer/seller fields).


What round4description.txt adds
--------------------------------
Round 4 keeps the same products and position limits as Round 3, but Trade.buyer
and Trade.seller are populated with participant names. You can identify every
other participant in the market and study their behavior on the public tape.

So the old “insider” question — who is leaning on the tape — can move from
inference from size / regime / Sonic-style gates (Round 3 scripts such as
identify_vev_insider_fingerprint_r3.py, extract informed-flow evidence, etc.)
to direct labeling of counterparties on each print.


Initial analysis (ROUND_4 days 1–3)
------------------------------------
Tape: Prosperity4Data/ROUND_4/trades_round_4_day_{1,2,3}.csv (semicolon-separated).

Only seven named participants appear: Mark 01, 14, 22, 38, 49, 55, 67 — a small
fixed cast of bots, consistent with the “Hello, I’m Mark” framing.

Flow roles (lot sums over all symbols in this slice):
  • Mark 01: very net buyer (+4678 lots bought vs sold as seller).
  • Mark 22: very net seller (−5477) — natural liquidity sink on the other side
    of many prints.
  • Mark 14 ↔ Mark 38: large two-way flow (714 vs 728 prints in opposite
    directions) — paired-bot behavior on HYDROGEL_PACK (each side ~50% buyer /
    seller share).
  • Mark 67 → Mark 49 and Mark 67 → Mark 22 appear as smaller but sharp directed
    motifs.

Dominant directed pair:
  • Mark 01 → Mark 22 is the single most common buyer–seller pair (1339 rows),
    consistent with basket / synchronized voucher selling. Multi-symbol bursts
    at one timestamp often show Mark 22 as seller (and sometimes Mark 01).

Same-timestamp bursts:
  • Up to 10 prints at one (day, timestamp) across VEV_4000, … — Mark 22 (and
    sometimes Mark 01). That reads as cross-product bot ladders, not human
    one-offs.

Per-product concentration (from the same three-day slice):
  • VELVETFRUIT_EXTRACT: Mark ~55 ~43% on both buy and sell sides — balanced
    market-maker style participation.
  • HYDROGEL_PACK: Mark 38 vs Mark 14 split ~50/50 on buyer and seller —
    two-sided bot duopoly.
  • VEV_5300: ~80% of buys from Mark 01, ~99% of sells from Mark 22 — almost a
    dedicated pipe; useful for “who is the forced seller at the touch” logic.

Connection to Round 3 “insider” intuition:
  Round 3 inferred structured / possibly informed flow from public fields only.
  Round 4 makes the same idea explicit: much of the structure is named bot
  choreography you can cluster and condition on (pair identity, bursts,
  per-symbol who always sells).
