Suggested direction — Round 4 research / trading
==================================================

Phase 1 — With counterparty IDs (Round 4)
------------------------------------------

Participant-level alpha / predictiveness
For each name U, tag prints where buyer==U or seller==U, then measure forward mid move (or markout) over 5/20/100 ticks, conditional on product, spread regime, time-of-day. Persistently positive mean markout for one side (e.g. always buying before up-moves) flags informed-style buying.

Deviation from “bot baseline”
Cluster Marks into roles (e.g. 01→22 basket seller, 14↔38 hydro duopoly). Define expected forward move after a print given (buyer,seller,symbol,regime). Names whose residual (actual − bot-conditional mean) is large and stable look like the interesting agent (if any human-like ID appears) or a mis-specified bot you can fade.

Sequence / graph methods
Build a directed graph buyer→seller weighted by count and notional; detect motifs (triads, repeated two-step chains). “Insider-like” often shows up as short paths from flow into a concentrated seller/buyer before large moves in another product (lead–lag).

Burst structure
Same-timestamp multi-symbol prints: attribute to orchestrating party (e.g. buyer across names). If bursts precede systematic moves in extract or core strikes, that party is your candidate scheduler, not necessarily “insider” but structural.

Adverse selection vs inventory
If you can approximate inventory (from own fills + visible pressure), test whether prints from party U hurt the passive side more when U is aggressive — classic informed trading signature (harder without full book history; easier in sim with full tapes).


Phase 2 — Identifying other tradeable edges (often more stable than “insider”)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Named-bot exploitation (Round 4–specific)
Condition quotes/fades on (buyer,seller) and burst signatures (e.g. post Mark 01 → Mark 22 basket: mean-revert vs trend depending on follow-through stats).

Microstructure
Queue position, touch changes, full-book microprice vs mid, spread regimes, trade-through frequency — edges that survive without any “insider.”

Cross-instrument lead–lag
Extract vs voucher wings vs hydro; Granger-style or simple lagged correlation of signed flow — works well when bots run fixed pipelines.

Regime splits
Same signal only in tight spread / high depth / first hour — reduces overfit and often separates bot schedules from noise.

Vol / smile mispricing
Your R3 BS + smile residual work; in R4 you can ask whether certain Marks’ prints cluster when smile is steepest (systematic pressure on wings).

Execution / adverse selection
When to take vs quote: use counterparty identity to not provide liquidity right before historically bad markouts for that pair.

Inventory-aware MM
Skew quotes when your position + observed pressure from a repeatedly selling Mark align.

    fewer events (n drops) but ~2× better per-event edge and cleaner signal
    (fewer false triggers).
